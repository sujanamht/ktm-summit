Overview

The Everest Base Camp Trek is one of the world's most iconic Himalayan journeys, taking you through the legendary Khumbu region of Nepal to stand at the foot of the highest mountain on Earth. At 5,364 metres, Everest Base Camp commands panoramic views of Khumbu Icefall and the soaring peaks of Lhotse, Nuptse, and Pumori.

The route follows the ancient Sherpa trading trail from Lukla through Namche Bazaar, Tengboche, Dingboche, and Lobuche. Along the way you will experience vibrant Sherpa culture, centuries-old monasteries, and some of the most dramatic mountain scenery in the world. The itinerary is carefully paced with acclimatisation days to give your body the best chance of adjusting safely to altitude.

Highlights

Day-by-Day Itinerary

1 Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m)
Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport. You will be met by our team and transferred to your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring Thamel's lively streets or resting. Evening welcome briefing and trek overview with your guide. (Meals: —)
2 Fly Kathmandu → Lukla; Trek to Phakding (2,610m)
Early morning flight to Lukla (2,840m) — one of the world's most dramatic airstrips. Begin trekking through rhododendron and pine forests along the Dudh Koshi River to Phakding. Walking time approx. 3–4 hours. (Meals: B, L, D)
3 Phakding → Namche Bazaar (3,440m)
A longer day with some steep ascent. Cross several suspension bridges over the Dudh Koshi, including the famous Hillary Bridge. First views of Everest from a ridge above Namche. Arrive at the bustling Sherpa capital. Walking time approx. 5–6 hours. (Meals: B, L, D)
4 Acclimatisation Day — Namche Bazaar
Rest day to acclimatise. Hike to Everest View Hotel (3,880m) for spectacular mountain panoramas, then return to Namche. Explore the colourful Saturday market (if applicable), Sherpa Culture Museum, and local shops. Acclimatisation day — (Meals: B, L, D)
5 Namche Bazaar → Tengboche (3,867m)
Trek through beautiful rhododendron and birch forests with stunning close-up views of Ama Dablam, Thamserku, and Kantega. Descend to the Dudh Koshi, cross a bridge, and climb to Tengboche. Visit the famous Tengboche Monastery. Walking time approx. 5–6 hours. (Meals: B, L, D)
Days 6–15: Dingboche → Acclimatisation → Lobuche → Gorak Shep → Everest Base Camp → Kala Patthar → Pheriche → Namche → Lukla → Fly to Kathmandu
All days follow the same accordion format.

You should be able to walk 5–7 hours per day on uneven terrain for multiple consecutive days. Prior trekking experience is helpful but not mandatory. We recommend cardiovascular training (hiking, running, cycling) for at least 2–3 months before departure.
The trek uses teahouses (mountain lodges) along the trail. Rooms are simple twin-share with basic facilities. At lower altitudes teahouses are more comfortable; at higher elevations (Gorak Shep) facilities are more basic. We book the best available teahouses at each stop.
